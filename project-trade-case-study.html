Project Trade Case Study

Project Snapshot

Project Trade is an NHL decision-support analytics project centered on evaluating trade and replacement scenarios with salary-cap awareness.

Current evidence base:

  • 23 total artifacts
  • 14 DOCX, 3 PPTX, 6 TWBX
  • Coverage across 4 lifecycle stages

Stage distribution:

  • Framing and Requirements: 4
  • Model Build and ETL: 10
  • Visuals and Recommendation: 8
  • Finalization: 1

The Challenge

Hockey trade decisions are high-impact and often influenced by fragmented views of player value, salary constraints, and fit.

The project challenge is to transform those decisions into a repeatable, transparent workflow that combines performance signals, weighting logic, and financial context.

The Goal

Build a practical analytics chain that supports two audiences at once:

  • technical builders who need traceable ETL and model logic,
  • decision makers who need clear recommendations and facilitation materials.

Approach

  1. Frame the decision context

    Early artifacts define the opportunity statement, stakeholder audience, data requirements, and known risks.

  2. Build and iterate the model

    Design documents, input-mapping artifacts, and multiple Tableau workbook iterations establish and refine weighted trade analysis behavior.

  3. Operationalize through ETL and demos

    Implementation and demo assets document how to build, run, and interpret the model workflow.

  4. Convert analysis into recommendations

    Visual design notes, recommendation reports, summary presentations, and training guides convert technical output into decision-ready guidance.

  5. Close with improvement planning

    A final improvement plan identifies enhancement priorities for model clarity and usefulness.

What the Artifact Set Demonstrates

  • Iterative model development rather than one-pass analysis.
  • Strong connection between business framing and technical execution.
  • Explicit handoff from analysis artifacts to recommendation communication.
  • Reusability through training and facilitation documentation.

Portfolio Value

As a portfolio project, Project Trade demonstrates end-to-end analytics operations:

  • stakeholder-aware framing,
  • data and modeling discipline,
  • iterative Tableau-based prototyping,
  • executive communication and adoption support.

Key Takeaway

Project Trade is not only a model artifact collection.

It is a full decision-support workflow that links framing, data preparation, model iteration, and recommendation delivery into a coherent operating narrative.

Suggested Presentation Flow

  1. Open with framing artifacts (steps 01 to 04).
  2. Show model design and iteration progression (05a, 05b, and 05d workbooks).
  3. Cover ETL and demo enablement (06A and 06B).
  4. Walk through visuals and recommendation outputs (07A to 08D).
  5. Close with the final improvement plan (step 10).
